# Portfolio positioning

This is the public-safe positioning layer distilled from private career-grounding notes,
the Microsoft claims register, current project artifacts, and the Behance research archive.

The private grounding source is not a publishable biography. It contains disputed numbers,
internal work context, personal material, and interpretations. Only high-confidence,
professionally relevant signals belong in the portfolio.

## What a hiring manager should understand

1. **Aadith can work inside complicated AI systems.** His Microsoft work connects response
   hierarchy, citations, references, evaluation, accessibility, design systems, and
   implementation detail.
2. **He turns subjective design judgment into reusable evidence.** His work includes
   interviews, criteria, examples, rubrics, prototype comparisons, and quality checks.
3. **He works beyond static handoff.** He uses code prototypes and contributes low-risk
   presentation-layer fixes with engineering review.
4. **He is comfortable with ambiguity and multiple disciplines.** Early-stage work,
   enterprise product design, visual storytelling, and founder responsibilities form a
   coherent practice rather than unrelated hobbies.
5. **He credits teams and names uncertainty.** The portfolio should remain specific about
   individual contribution without pretending complex work happened alone.

## What a founder or investor should understand

1. **Owly is a product thesis, not only an AI-video demo.** Its differentiation is reusable
   brand context, progressive control, selective regeneration, and a repeatable production
   workflow.
2. **The team has complementary capabilities.** Aadith leads product/design and customer
   storytelling; his co-founders cover AI systems plus backend and operational execution.
3. **The company has crossed from idea into paid learning.** Use the conservative public
   floor of 8+ early paid customers. Do not publish disputed revenue or MRR figures.
4. **The current service layer is intentional but temporary.** Hands-on work exposes
   repeated customer decisions that can become software; the long-term model is reusable
   creative infrastructure.
5. **Aadith understands that focus and repeat demand matter more than accelerator logos.**
   Avoid using applications or interviews as substitutes for traction.

## Core narrative

> Aadith designs the parts of AI that need judgment after generation: evidence, control,
> evaluation, accessibility, and the last mile into a usable product.

The supporting identities reinforce that narrative:

- **Enterprise designer:** learns how quality survives scale, governance, and many owners.
- **Founder:** learns how customer urgency, pricing, and repeatability change product choices.
- **Builder:** makes flows tangible in code before the room becomes attached to a static idea.
- **Endurance cyclist:** provides credible evidence of long-horizon commitment, not a metaphor
  for every design decision.

## Publication boundaries

Do not publish:

- Compensation, level, promotion, performance-review, or manager information.
- Internal Microsoft names, implementation details, metrics, rollouts, colleagues, or links.
- Health, relationship, financial, family, or other private personal material.
- Exact Owly revenue/MRR until checked against primary records.
- Accelerator applications or interviews as though they were funding or admission.
- Conflicting award, academic-rank, cycling-distance, or employment-date versions.
- Interpretive strengths or blind spots as objective psychological facts.
